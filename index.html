Work

Information Architecture

Information architecture is a great way to find all the ways in which a user can navigate through in our product. Find an example below of a sample IA that I created.
I make sure before wireframing and prototyping (for which Figma, XD are a great tool) that Information Architecture has been mapped for the client. This gives a good clarity in moving forward with product/feature development.

Product Requirements Documentation

PRD reports are centric to a product managers daily workloads. This is a sample Product requirements documentation that I created as part of an assignment, where I am trying to address a problem at hand for reduced check out rates!
To understand product requirements, mapping a good customer persona and then documenting the journey map is centric to moving forward with the prd. Here I have mapped Customer Journey Map of a Ecommerce Consumer while jotting down all features that we can add for each step in the journey.

Product Roadmap

Product roadmaps can be defined as part of features, themes and user stories that can be planned in various sprints with the product team and managed. I created a consistent backlog where every feature had a user story with tasks and subtasks and I created a gannt chart that served as both product roadmap and activity manager.

Features Roadmap

Feature roadmaps track the development and releases of a product's key features. They communicate specifically how a product will evolve, without going into detail on other areas of a business. This keeps the focus on initiatives that add value for users. I created a sample document for an ecommerce company with the following features.

User Stories

During SLDC, user stories were created as per agile framework to support design, devlopment and research phases. User stories also acted as a crude documentation for the client.

Process Map

This one is my favorite. I have worked with a dozen of clients who donot understand either: 1)How to explain processes to existing/new employees 2)How to implement effciency by measuring gaps in their processes? 3) Dccumenting processes after automation is planned to be introduced.
Process mapping has proved to be such an important too for this analysis. Flowcharts, BPMN, IA etc are indispensable for this process. Find above an example of a process map for a sales process.

Business process management notation

BPMN is essentially a form of Process mapping, but it found out to be a more precise title when information systems were allowed. I did modelling in softwares like Flowable, Bonitasoft, Camunda, kissflow etc.( primarily bonitasoft).

Logic Design

For example, Saas company like Urban Clap that assigns sellers dynamically to buyers like event management. As a business analyst I had this prevelige to work on design logic for backend for applications.
